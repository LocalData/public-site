Fighting blight with data

The Gary-Harris Initiative was formed in partnership between the City of Chicago's former Mayor Richard M. Daley, Gary's current Mayor Karen Freeman-Wilson and the University of Chicago's Harris School of Public Policy. Gary, Indiana is a city that like many smaller American communities experienced a drastic population decline from 1960 - 2010. Thousands of abandoned or near-abandoned buildings require a $10-15k budget for a single demolition.

67 volunteers

Though the city recognized a need for redevelopment and revitalization, a significant lack of data was available on the condition and current ownership of abandoned properties. The best administrative datasets available from the Tax Assessor's office was out of date and didn't provide condition information about each property.

11,651 properties surveyed
2,000 acres covered

Results

With 67 volunteers, the team surveyed over 11,651 properties covering 2,000 acres across the city. Statistics were created to understanding both the spatial concentration of vacancy and abandonment, target areas for demolition or deconstruction and where the city should concentrate efforts to board up distressed housing.
