Use Cases

LocalData has been used across the globe to map and track critical information about cities and residents. Dozens of projects have benefited from the information, from mapping vacant property to informing future bike lane and transportation policy. Here's just a few ways LocalData has been used by researchers, public sector practitioners, and nonprofit organizations

Mapping Digital Access in Quito

The MIT Media Lab City Science Group collected data with MIT graduate researchers on existing digital infrastructure in the first UNESCO world heritage site in Quito, Ecuador. Researchers gathered information on the ground about existing access to wifi hotspots, internet connectivity and the cost of connecting. Results were used to inform research on the future of designing an Innovation District in line with economic development goals set to revitalize the downtown core.

Collecting Vacant Property Data in Gary, Indiana

The Mayor's office in Gary, Indiana undertook a massive campaign, collecting data on over 30,000 buildings. They targeted blighted neighborhood properties, focusing on those in close proximity to schools and other amenities, measuring the impact of dangerous decaying infrastructure on quality of life. This data informed municipal policy and allocate federal funding for community revitalization.

Bicycle Counts: Encouraging Healthy Choices in Southeast Michigan

The Community Foundation of Southeast Michigan collected and mapped hundreds of data points on the existing demographic usage of bicycles in Southeast Michigan. Data from the study helped inform a Greenway Initiative, so that data-driven bicycle infrastructure recommendations can encourage healthy behavior among residents.
