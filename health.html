Use Cases

LocalData has been used across the globe to map and track critical information about the environment, informing community health assessment. Here's just a few ways LocalData has been used by researchers, public sector practitioners, and nonprofit organizations

Bicycle Counts: Encouraging Healthy Choices

The Community Foundation of Southeast Michigan collected and mapped hundreds of points of data on the existing demographic usage of bicycles in Southeast Michigan. Data from the study helped inform a future Greenway Initiative, so that data-driven bicycle infrastructure recommendations can encourage healthy behavior among residents.

Neighborhood Safety: Quality of Life + Community Health

The Mayor's office in Gary, Indiana undertook a massive campaign collecting data on over 30,000 buildings, targeting blighted properties in neighborhoods in close proximity to schools and other amenities to measure the impact of dangerous decaying infrastructure on quality of life. This data was used to inform municipal policy and allocate federal funding for community revitalization.
