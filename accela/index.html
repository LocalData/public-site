Need to assess urban infrastructure?
LocalData + Accela can help.

LocalData is a cloud-hosted mapping platform that allows civic organizations (governments and non-profits) to quickly collect data and visualize online. Add data to existing municipal datasets or create new civic data that feeds directly back into municipal databases.

Design surveys + collect real-time data

Design custom surveys online that fit the needs of your organization, then collect data using any smartphone or tablet with a browser. Add data to existing base files like parcels, road lines or other shapes, or create data by dropping points on a map. Rich geospatial information is stored online including photos, qualitative and quantitative data.

Data dashboard + integration

Once data is entered, LocalData visualizes patterns on a map, letting you drill down to the parcel or point level in the web browser. Data you collect can instantly connect to existing datasets stored in Accela, including valuable information like inspection reports and ownership information. Generating Civic Data

LocalData can be used to produce, map and understand many kinds of civic data. Here are a few of the most popular use cases:

Supply chain + logistics mapping

Researchers at the MIT Megacities Logistics Lab used LocalData to collect and map data on supply chain logistics in Mexico City, Singapore and Rio. Traffic counts and street-level observations on retail deliveries allowed researchers and local policymakers to create better traffic laws supporting local and global goods distribution.

Vacancy + blight mapping

Gary, Indiana Office of the Mayor used LocalData to map and understand blighted properties owned by the city. Data was used to secure federal funding from Obama's Hardest Hit funds and official strategy for future demolition with up-to-date information on at-risk neighborhoods.

Bike infrastructure

The Community Foundation of Southeast Michigan used LocalData to map and understand existing bicycle use throughout the region. The data collected was used to make decisions about where to place bike lanes and hubs as part of a $15 million regional greenway initiative.

CivicID powers engagement

These tools aren't just available to city governments. Thanks to the Construct API and Accela's new CivicID, nonprofits and others working in an Accela-powered city can access the same data, as well as discover new ways to contribute data back to the city.
